Oyo State at a Glance

Oyo! Pace-Setter State

Oyo is a south-western state in Nigeria, with its capital is Ibadan, it is bounded in the north by Kwara State, in the east by Osun State, in the south by Ogun State and to the west partly by Ogun State and partly by the Republic of Benin. It consists of 33 Local Government Areas.

People & Culture

The state which is homogeneous is predominantly occupied by Yoruba people. Within the State, however, there are sub-ethnic groups with distinct dialect peculiarities. Agriculture is the main occupation of the native people of Oyo State. They grow crops like maize, yam, cassava, millet, rice, plantains, cocoa, palm produce, cashew, etc.

Geography & Climate

The topography of the State is of gentle rolling low land in the South, rising to a plateau of about 40metres. The State is well-drained with rivers flowing from the upland in the North-South direction. Oyo State has an equatorial climate with dry and wet seasons and relatively high humidity. The dry season lasts from November to March while the wet season starts from April and ends in October.
